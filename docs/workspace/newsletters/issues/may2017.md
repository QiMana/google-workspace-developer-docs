# may2017

G Suite Developer Newsletter May 2017 Build with G Suite With I/O 2017 officially wrapped up, here's a quick reminder on the many ways in which developers can build for G Suite. From App Maker to our APIs to Gmail Add-ons, there's never been a better time to be a G Suite developer! Learn more Watch the App Maker Session from I/O 2017 If you weren't able to make it in person at I/O 2017, you can view our App Maker session featuring a brand new demo here. Watch the recap How Machine Learning in G Suite makes people more productive There's been a lot of talk about machine learning lately, and it's a concept the G Suite team has taken to heart when building its products. Learn more here API & Tooling News Announced: Updated Developer Identity Guidelines and Registration Processes to Protect Users—These new guidelines cover the app publishing process, risk assessment systems, and user-facing consent page in order to better detect spoofed or misleading application identities. Launched: New Google Drive Metrics Now Accessible from Reports API—The new metrics methods provide greater visibility into how files are shared inside and outside of domain. Launched: Updates to End User Consent for 3rd-party Apps and Single Sign-on Providers—We are implementing a new redirect form to make it clear to users which account they are authenticating with, as well as the permissions they are granting to applications. Launched: A New Issue Tracker for G Suite Developers—The tracker allows for improved collaboration and transparency by enabling developers to see the issues that Google is actively working on. Announced: Gmail Add-ons Developer Preview —A new way for developers to bring the power of their app into Gmail with just one tap, across devices. Developer News Added nine apps to the G Suite pre-integrated SSO catalog: Asana, Dialpad, Evernote Business, Expensify, Keeper, Lucidchart, Pagerduty, RingCentral, and Trello.
