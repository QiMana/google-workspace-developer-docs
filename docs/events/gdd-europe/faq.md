# Frequently asked questions

### General

2017 Dates & Location

The 2017 Google Developer Days Europe will be held by Google Poland sp. z o.o. with its business address at Emilii Plater 53, 00-113 Warsaw, Poland, on 5-6 September 2017 at ICE Congress Centre, Marii Konopnickiej 17, 30-302 Kraków, Poland.

Content Formats

Throughout the event, in person attendees will be able to participate by attending sessions, getting hands-on in the sandbox and codelabs, chatting with Googlers at office hours, and more.

Livestream & Recordings

If you’re unable to attend Google Developer Days Europe in person, watch the livestream of the keynotes and all sessions on the [Google Developer Days Europe 2017 website](http://developers.google.com/events/gdd-europe/) throughout the event. Sessions will be recorded and available after the event on our [Google Developers YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/GoogleDevelopers).

Extended Events

Extended events happen around the world and are hosted by local developers during Google Developer Days Europe. At these events, organizers may livestream the event and host their own sessions including codelabs, demos, etc. If you’re joining us as a Google Developer Days Europe Extended host this year, here are the steps you should take to get started:

- Read through the [Organizer Guide](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/e/2PACX-1vQW-SSCQKAn5rMs0NWXtsSLCXYUOMBGMZJgu5PiqXyqsX8SLJ5dqaJ7w4cbNXdIrufnEVVLDTR8gV6X/pub?start=false&loop=false&delayms=3000&slide=id.g1e96f7f4d4_1_0) to get tips and suggestions about hosting a successful event.
- [Register](https://services.google.com/fb/forms/gdd-extended/) your public event on the Google Developer Days Europe website for increased visibility.
- Use the official #GDDEurope hashtag on all your social posts related to Google Developer Days Europe 2017 Extended for easier discoverability.

For questions about the Google Developer Days Europe Extended program and/or to request the deletion of their personal and/or event data after the event ends, contact us at [gdd-europe-extended@google.com](mailto:gdd-europe-extended@google.com).

### Attendance Details

All presentations at Google Developer Days Europe will be in English. Attendance at sessions, codelabs, app reviews and office hours during Google Developer Days Europe is on a first-come, first-served basis.

Pro Tips

If you’re joining us in person, here are some useful tips to navigate your way through the conference:

- **Bike parking:** This is complimentary for attendees and will be available outside of the venue during event hours. Please note that there is only enough parking for 30 bicycles. Parking will be on a first-come, first-served basis.
- **Lost & Found:** For any lost and found, please report to the help desk near registration where any items handed in will be kept safely.
- **Meals:** Attendees are offered complimentary breakfast, lunch and snacks on both days of the conference.
- **After Party:** One night you don’t want to miss! Attendees are invited to enjoy food, drinks, music, games, and more during the evening of 5 September at ICE Congress. Your attendee badge is required for entrance.

Badges & Badge Pick-up

Badge pickup will be available starting at 08:00 on Tuesday 5 September. Make sure to bring your photo ID along with your registration confirmation email, as we’ll need to verify it prior to assigning you your Google Developer Days Europe badge. We’re OK with government issued licenses, passports, and other forms of identification. If you don’t have proper identification, you won’t be able to receive a badge and won’t be admitted into the conference.

Everyone is guaranteed a seat for the keynotes but the best seats will be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis. So make sure to arrive to the event on the earlier side!

Please note, you may not share, give, or otherwise provide your badge to anyone. Google Developer Days Europe badges aren’t replaceable, so don't lose yours or you won’t be readmitted to the conference. You must wear your Google Developer Days Europe badge to gain admission to Google Developer Days Europe, including the sessions, developer sandbox, and after hours. If requested by security, please display or provide additional identification. Google Developer Days Europe badges may include your name and company or organization (if provided).

After Party

All registered attendees are invited to the after party on the evening of 5 September. Event credentials will be required for entry. Please join us for food, drinks, music, games, and more. Alcoholic beverages will be available for those 21 and over.

Event Attire

Google Developer Days Europe is an indoor developer event, so please be comfortable and casual. There is no enforced dress code. There will be a coat check available near registration for your convenience.

Accessibility Accommodations for Attendees

Google strives to make events open and accessible to everyone, regardless of disability or special needs. Participants with disabilities and/or special needs should provide details in the registration form and/or by emailing us at [gdd-europe@google.com](mailto:gdd-europe@google.com). This information will be kept private and will be distributed only to the individuals who need to know it to complete your accommodation request. Otherwise, please approach the Help Desk at Google Developer Days Europe if you need assistance or have questions about accessibility during the event.

ICE Congress Center is an accessible venue. There are three lifts that allow disabled access to all floors inside the venue, and is a flat surface to enter the building. Service dogs will be allowed into the building.

We’ll provide live real-time transcription (CART) for the keynotes and some of our sessions. Caption text displayed on a large screen at the front of the room will be accessible to all attendees. We’ll also use the text to provide captions for our livestream and post-event recordings.

Accessible parking spaces will be available for those who need them. When arriving to the venue, please follow signs for Accessible parking and have your placard hanging from the car mirror and visible to parking employees at all times to expedite the parking process. Accessible parking spaces are readily available on a first-come, first-served basis and may not be reserved in advance to fairly serve all attendees.

No Soliciting

No solicitation or selling of items or services is allowed at Google Developer Days Europe. Any attendee conducting these activities may be removed from the event.

Youth Attendance

Attendees must be at least 15 years of age to attend Google Developer Days Europe. Anyone ages 15-17 must have a signed parental/guardian consent form in order to attend without a parent or guardian present. Please contact [gdd-europe@google.com](mailto:gdd-europe@google.com) prior to registration to obtain a form.
